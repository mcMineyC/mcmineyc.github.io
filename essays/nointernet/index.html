How My Life Would Change If The Internet Ceased To Exist

  My life would be very different if the internet ceased to exist. Since I self-teach myself to code, I use the internet a lot. Every day, I look something up on either Stack Overflow, W3Schools, Github, or the Mozilla Developer Network. While I could learn using books, the constantly changing nature of the technological world sometimes renders them useless, even as soon as they’re published.

  Another way my life would change without the internet is that I wouldn’t be able to learn how to make 3D animations using tutorials from YouTube channels like Grant Abbitt and CG Geek. Not only are videos helpful when doing complex modeling, but they are a necessity in knowing which buttons to click in which order.

  However, if the entire internet was going to go down, there would probably be signs leading up to it. Certain aspects of websites not working, some websites down entirely, constant errors, that sort of thing. In such a situation, I would make a complete offline copy of the websites I use the most frequently. But if a hacker, say, installed malware on every single server on the entire internet to completely wipe all data when triggered, then yes, the internet would cease to exist and there wouldn’t be time to make copies of the websites. In that case, I would be completely out of luck. Still, the possibility of that happening is highly unlikely.
